Favourite Places

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan (reigned from 1628 to 1658)

Hyderabad

Hyderabad is known as the City of Pearls, as it had once flourished as a global center for trade of rare diamonds, emeralds as well as natural pearls. Mecca Masjid, Charminar, Golconda fort, QutubShahi tombs, Paigah tombs are some of the historic structures.

Rajasthan

Rajasthan, meaning "Land of Kings," is India's largest state by area, situated in the northwest and renowned for its rich history, vibrant culture, majestic forts and palaces, and the vast Thar Desert, making it a major tourist destination with cities like Jaipur (the "Pink City") and Udaipur famous for their architectural marvels and royal heritage

Delhi

Delhi, the capital of India, is a bustling metropolis with a rich history, renowned for its iconic monuments like the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and India Gate, which reflect its past as a center of power for various dynasties;

Vizag

Visakhapatnam, commonly known as "Vizag," is a coastal city in Andhra Pradesh, India, renowned for its picturesque beaches, rich history, and significant naval presence, making it a major tourist destination on the Bay of Bengal

Kerala

Kerala, situated on India's southwest coast, is often called "God's Own Country" due to its stunning natural beauty, including lush green landscapes, serene backwaters, pristine beaches, and the Western Ghats mountain range, making it a prime tourist destination globally

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is considered to be the greatest architectural achievement in the whole range of Indo-Islamic architecture. Its recognised architectonic beauty has a rhythmic combination of solids and voids, concave and convex and light shadow; such as arches and domes further increases the aesthetic aspect. Not a piece of architecture, as other buildings are, but the proud passions of an emperor’s love wrought in living stones.

Hyderabad

Hyderabad, the City of Pearls, is a vibrant blend of tradition and modernity. Renowned for its iconic Charminar and historic Golconda Fort, it showcases a rich cultural heritage. The city is a culinary paradise, famous for its biryani and Irani chai. Hyderabad’s diverse population celebrates festivals like Diwali, Eid, and Bonalu with equal zeal. Its thriving IT sector coexists with old bazaars like Laad Bazaar. Hyderabad truly embodies the spirit of unity in diversity.
